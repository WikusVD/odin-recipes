French Toast:

Description:

Best Bread For French Toast:

The best breads for French toast are brioche, sourdough, French bread, or challah. These varieties are dense and sturdy enough to handle total saturation in the wet, milky, egg custard without falling apart. However, in a pinch, any thick-sliced white bread will do.

French toast is traditionally made with day-old slices because they absorb the eggy mixture better than fresh ones (and this method prevents waste it's a win-win). Of course, a slightly stale loaf is certainly not a requirement. If you only have fresh bread on hand, that'll work just fine.

Ingredients:

Every home cook has their own twist on basic French toast, but most variations contain the following ingredients:

  • ⅔ cup milk
  • 2x large eggs
  • 1x teaspoon of vanilla extract (optional)
  • ¼ teaspoon ground cinnamon or nutmeg (optional)
  • pinch of salt
  • 6x slices of bread
  • 1x tablespoon of unsalted butter

Directions:

  • Step 1: Gather all ingredients.
  • Step 2: Whisk milk, eggs, vanilla, cinnamon, and salt together in a shallow bowl until combined and smooth.
  • Step 3: Lightly butter a griddle or skillet and heat over medium-high heat. Soak each slice of bread in egg mixture, about 5 to 10 seconds per side, allowing it to absorb the mixture without becoming soggy.
  • Step 4: Transfer to the hot skillet and cook until golden brown and slightly crisp, 3 to 4 minutes per side.
  • Step 5: Serve hot and enjoy with butter and syrup.
